Cylindric Joint

The Cylindric joint object is used to represent a Cylindric joint in a FEDEM model.

A Cylindric joint consists of a slave triad sliding along a straight path defined by two or more master triads. The local coordinate system of the joint is defined with its Z-axis directed along the slide path. The X- and Y-axes are then defined from the coordinate systems of the master triads.

The Cylindric joint has four unconstrained DOFs that allow for translational displacement along the local Z-axis, and rotation about all three axes. It has two joint DOFs with property settings. They allow you to control the translational displacement of the slave triad relative to the first master triad along the local Z-axis (the slider variable), and the angle of rotation of the slave triad about the local Z-axis, respectively. The rotation angle is measured between the X-axis of the first master triad and the X-axis of the slave triad. It does not constrain motion in the other two rotational directions.

See the section on "Joints" in the "Mechanism Elements" chapter in the FEDEM User's Guide, for more information on Cylindric joints.
